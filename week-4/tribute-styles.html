Emily Dickinson c.1847 – Captured in quiet youth

Born:

December 10, 1830 — Amherst, Massachusetts

Education:

Attended Mount Holyoke Female Seminary for a short amount of time.

Life:

Lived mostly reclusively in her family home, where she wrote nearly 1,800 poems. Only a handful were published during her lifetime.

Died:

May 15, 1886 — Amherst, Massachusetts

Legacy:

Posthumously recognized as one of the most important American poets. Her work challenged traditional poetic form, especially in rhythm, punctuation, and theme.
